City of Chelsea
Cultural Asset
Mapping Project

The City of Chelsea is a community with a rich history, unique culture, and creative people. The City of Chelsea Cultural Asset Mapping Project aims to identify, celebrate, and protect the places and resources important to the City's identity, culture, or creativity.

You can use the button bellow to jump to the full version of the map, and explore which cultural assets have been identified. You can also take the survey, and help us put more cultural assets on the map. If you'd like to learn more about cultural assets and cultural asset mapping, you can scroll down to read more.

What is a cultural asset?

A cultural asset is something that contributes to the community's creativity, knowledge, traditions, and meaning. A cultural asset can be a physical thing, like a building or piece of art. It can also be something intangible and temporary, like an event, ritual, or ceremony. The City of Chelsea Cultural Asset Mapping Project currently uses the following cateogries to group and organize cultural assets, recognizing that other categories exist, and that some cultural assets might fit into more than one category.

Architecture

Historic buildings, significant restorations, contemporary buildings, notable design details

Landmarks

Heritage sites, landmarks, monuments, cemeteries

Public Art

Murals, street art, sculptures, art installations

Creative Industries

Creative studios, practice spaces, creative businesses, coworking facilities, art supplies, artist live/work space

Cultural facilities

Performance spaces, exhibitions spaces, places of worship, social clubs

Food

Restaurants, bars, commercial kitchens, urban agriculture

Events + Programming

Schools, libraries, educational programming, festivals, meeting halls, other notable events

Parks + Open Space

Parks, playgrounds, natural spaces, scenic areas, community gardens

And more

These categories serve as a guide, but the community will identify other types of cultural assets!
